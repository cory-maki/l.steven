Background

Four Decades in Labor and Employment Law

L. Steven Platt has practiced labor and employment and commercial litigation law for more than 40 years, representing employers of every size in disputes across the country. His work spans union negotiations and arbitrations, defense before the National Labor Relations Board, class action wage-and-hour litigation, and non-compete and trade-secret disputes.

Steven earned his J.D. from Loyola University Chicago School of Law and his B.A. from Northwestern University. He is licensed in Illinois and admitted to practice before numerous federal courts, and has handled labor and employment matters in multiple states and jurisdictions on behalf of national employers.

Recognition

Independently Verified Credentials

Steven Platt is a Fellow of the American College of Labor and Employment Lawyers, a distinction reserved for a small number of practitioners in the field. He is rated Preeminent, the highest tier, by Martindale-Hubbell in both peer and client review.

Chambers and Partners has ranked L. Steven Platt among the top five labor and employment lawyers in Chicago, and the American Bar Association has recognized him as a Lawyer of Distinction. Together, these credentials reflect evaluation by independent organizations rather than self-reported claims.

In the Press

A Trusted Voice on Workplace Law

Reporters covering labor and employment issues regularly turn to Steven Platt for perspective. He has been quoted as a subject-matter expert in the New York Times, USA Today, and the Chicago Tribune, as well as in legal trade press covering employment litigation and regulatory developments.

That media profile complements a career built on courtroom results and peer recognition — press citations from national outlets reflect editorial judgments about whose perspective adds clarity to complex workplace law questions.

Speaking & Teaching

Over 100 Presentations on Employment Law

Steven has given more than 100 presentations and training sessions to employers, in-house counsel, and fellow practitioners on topics including workplace harassment, discipline and discharge, enforcement of work rules, and avoiding discrimination claims. He lectures for the Illinois Institute for Continuing Legal Education, the National Employment Lawyers Association, and other professional organizations.

He is currently completing a chapter for an upcoming IICLE publication on employment leave practices, continuing a long record of contributing practical guidance to the employment law community rather than only appearing in it.

Practice Areas

How Steven Helps Employers

Steven's practice centers on defending employers in litigation and advising them before disputes start. That includes counseling on non-compete and restrictive covenant enforcement, guiding companies through internal workplace investigations, and drafting and defending employment arbitration agreements.

Whether the matter is a single-plaintiff discrimination claim or a nationwide class action, Steven's approach is the same: understand the business risk first, then build the legal strategy around it.
